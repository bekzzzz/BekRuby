Kuurdak is a traditional Kyrgyz dish of fried lamb or beef with onions, potatoes, and spices. Golden brown and hearty, itâ€™s a comforting favorite served hot with bread.

Samsa is a flaky pastry filled with seasoned lamb or beef, onions, and sometimes vegetables. Baked or tandoor-cooked, itâ€™s a savory snack with a satisfying crunch.

Plov

Plov is a classic Kyrgyz dish of aromatic rice cooked with carrots, onions, and meat, seasoned with garlic and spices. Rich and flavorful, itâ€™s a beloved one-pot meal for any occasion.

Besh barmak

Besh-barmak, meaning "five fingers," is a traditional Kyrgyz dish of boiled meat (usually horse or lamb) with noodles and broth, topped with onions. A cultural staple, itâ€™s served at celebrations and gatherings.

Kebab

Shashlyk is a Kyrgyz favorite of marinated lamb, beef, or chicken skewers grilled over an open flame. Tender and flavorful, itâ€™s served with bread and salads.

Dymdama

Dymdama is a layered dish of potatoes, carrots, onions, and lamb or beef, slow-cooked with spices for a hearty, flavorful meal. Itâ€™s a favorite at celebrations and family gatherings.

Manty

Manty are steamed Kyrgyz dumplings filled with minced lamb or beef and onions. Soft and juicy, theyâ€™re served with yogurt or sour cream as a hearty meal or snack.
