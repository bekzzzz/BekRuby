7 Days Best Of Kyrgyzstan

Day 1 - Pick up from Airport - Bishkek - Ala-Archa

Take some time to rest. After lunch, you'll transfer to Ala-Archa National Park, which is located 40 km away. There, you'll find yourself in an alpine gorge through which the fast-flowing Ala-Archa River runs. This gorge is flanked by tall, snow-covered peaks with steep-sided, forested mountain slopes.

Overnight: Freedom hotel, My hotel or Futuro

Day 2 - Bishkek- Chon Kemin

(148 km â€“ driving)

After breakfast we will arrive at the Chon Kemin Guest house. You will embark on a horseback riding excursion to Chon Kemin gorge, which will take approximately 2-3 hours.Lunch and Dinner in the guest house.

Overnight:Jekshen guest house

Day 3 - Chon Kemin â€“ Grigorievka gorge - Karakol

(~300 km â€“ driving)

After breakfast, we'll drive to Karakol city in the Issyk-Kul region. In Cholpon-Ata city, you can enjoy the sight of the second largest alpine lake and have lunch. Following lunch, we'll continue our journey to Karakol city. Along the way, we'll make stops at Grigorevka and Semenovka gorges, which are known for hosting the World Nomad Games. Our guide will prepare coffee for you, offering a beautiful view of the gorge. Dinner will be served at the ethno restaurant Dastorkon.

Overnight: Muras or Green Yard guest house

Day 4 - Hiking in Altyn Arashan

(~30 km â€“ driving)

Today, you will travel to the Altyn-Arashan mountain gorge. You'll reach the place using a special off-road truck designed for passengers (Ural). The road follows the Arashan River, ascending the hills until you reach the Arashan hot spring thermal bath valley. Take in the natural beauty and enjoy a wilderness lunch. You'll also have the opportunity to hike to the hot spring. The terrain is relatively easy to traverse, and the hike typically takes about 4 hours at an average walking speed.

Overnight: lodge, Elza.

(~160 km â€“ driving) (~3 km â€“trekking)

Today, the Russian military car will pick us up and take us back to the city where we will continue our trip. We'll be driving along the south shore of Issyk-Kul Lake, the second-largest alpine lake in the world after Titicaca in Peru. Today, you'll visit Fairy tail canyons and witness the striking red rock of Jeti-Oguz, set against the backdrop of snow-capped mountains.

Overnight: Local guest house

( 260 km â€“ driving)

Having enjoyed breakfast, you will be transferred to the sparkling, crystal-clear mountain lake, Song-Kol, passing through Kochkor village. In the village, you can visit a local family for lunch and participate in a workshop to create traditional handmade felt carpets. It's definitely worth a try! After lunch, we'll continue the journey to Song-Kol, crossing the high pass Kalmak-Ashuu (3400m). This pass will lead us to the northern lakeshore, where you will be welcomed in a yurt camp. Here, you'll dine and sleep in the yurt camp.

Overnight: Yurt Camp

(250kmâ€“driving)

Following this adventure, you will transfer to Bishkek. On the way, you'll visit the Burana Tower architectural complex, which dates back to the 10th and 11th centuries and played a significant role on the Great Silk Road. The complex is situated 15 km from the main road. Lunch will be served at the Dunghan family house. From there, you will be transported back to Bishkek, where you'll have some free time to explore. In the evening, a farewell dinner at a local restaurant will be awaiting you.

Overnight: Freedom hotel, My hotel or Futuro

Food

Kuurdak

Kuurdak is a traditional Kyrgyz dish featuring fried meat (often lamb or beef) with onions, potatoes, and spices. Cooked until golden brown, it offers hearty flavors and textures. Served hot, it's a beloved comfort food, enjoyed with bread, showcasing Kyrgyzstan's rich culinary heritage in a single dish.

Samsy

Samsa is a popular Kyrgyz dish, consisting of flaky pastry filled with seasoned meat (typically lamb or beef), onions, and sometimes vegetables. Baked or cooked in a tandoor, it offers a savory flavor and a satisfying crunch. Samsa is enjoyed as a snack or meal, reflecting Kyrgyz cuisine's diversity.

Plov

Plov is a staple dish in Kyrgyz cuisine, featuring aromatic rice cooked with carrots, onions, and often meat (such as lamb, beef, or chicken). Seasoned with garlic, cumin, and other spices, it offers a rich and flavorful one-pot meal. Plov is enjoyed on special occasions and as everyday comfort food.

Besh barmak

Besh-barmak is a traditional Kyrgyz dish, translating to "five fingers," symbolizing the traditional way of eating it with one's hands. It consists of boiled meat (often horse or lamb) and homemade noodles, served with broth and accompanied by onions. Besh-barmak holds cultural significance, often served during celebrations and gatherings.

Kebab

Shashlyk is a popular Kyrgyz dish consisting of skewered and grilled chunks of marinated meat, commonly lamb, beef, or chicken. Marinated in a mixture of spices, herbs, it's cooked over an open flame, resulting in tender, flavorful kebabs. Shashlyk is often served with bread and salads.

Dymdama

Dymdama is a traditional Kyrgyz dish consisting of layered potatoes, carrots, onions, and meat (often lamb or beef), seasoned with salt, pepper, and other spices. Cooked slowly in a heavy pot, it's steamed to create a flavorful and hearty meal, often enjoyed during celebrations and family gatherings.

Manty

Manty is a popular Kyrgyz dumpling dish filled with seasoned minced meat (often lamb or beef) and onions. The dumplings are typically steamed, resulting in a soft, doughy exterior and juicy, flavorful filling. Manty is often served with a side of yogurt or sour cream and enjoyed as a hearty meal or snack.
